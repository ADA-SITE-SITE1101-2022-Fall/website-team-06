The 25â€“30 fourth graders of Baku Secondary School No. 276 attended our team's presentation on ADA University and the field of computer science.

After giving a PPT presentation and responding to some of the children's questions relating to university life, we moved on to solving Code.org puzzles. Using a brief yet engaging game, we attempted to explain the fundamentals of programming to kids following the presentation.

After a brief introduction, we placed our laptops between two or three students and they each started working on their own puzzles. After the "Angry Birds" algorithms, we played "Zombie VS. Plants" before moving on to the "Ice Age" puzzles.

The majority of students showed interest in solving these puzzles and did so with a good level of comprehension. One of us would offer our assistance to pupils when they were having problems or challenges. We would like to thank the staff of School No. 276 and the fourth graders for their assistance in making this event a success.

This event was beneficial for us as well as the students of this school in terms of learning new information. We acquired several significant experiences, such as public speaking, our first practical assignment outside of the class, and the capacity to teach our understanding of the codes to others.
